# Eyes Are The Window To The Soul

This is an older story. It was from a time that I was still studying in university, so it is at least 10 years ago. I was doing a minor at the time called the educational minor. It is one semester in which you study to become a high school teacher. Part of this minor is a class that didn't have a specific topic, but it was more general about reflecting on your lessons and talking about situations that happened in the classroom. Part of this class was to give one of your prepared lessons so you could get feedback from your classmates. But there was a twist. The teacher would assign some classmates with some role to play in the practice lesson, like the annoying kid that keeps asking questions or a shy kid that would talk way too soft to be heard.

So one day, we would do a practice lesson and it was the turn of Sophie. She left the room, so the teacher could assign roles. Today, the teacher came up with the role of annoying kid that would try to disrupt the lesson. Who did he choose for this role? Me! I was so excited about this. I already had all these plans of pulling the hair of the girl next to me or flipping the table. This role was made for me. But... there was a restriction: if she would demand that I stop with confidence and authority, then I would need to stop. This was still a class, so this would teach Sophie how to handle children in class.

Sophie was called back in and she started her lesson. On the table next to me there was this pen case of my classmate. While Sophie was with her back to the table, I knocked it off the table. Sophie turns around, picks up the case and puts it back on the table. She is not phased at all and just continues with her story. She turns around and I quickly knock it off the table again. She says: Rico don't do that. I was like: that wasn't me, she did that. It was not a very convincing lie. She puts the case back on the table. This was not authoritative enough in my eyes, so I could continue. She turns around and I knock the case off the table for the third time. She turns to me and I look her in the eyes. And then... in her eyes... I see that she... breaks.

I cannot explain in words how you can see that in someone's eyes, but I did. A few seconds later, she starts crying and runs out of the classroom. The teacher goes after her, we are all in shock and are like what just happened? The teacher comes back after a while and tells us Sophie is not doing well, it was not my fault, and the lesson is done for today.

There is this expression that I heard somewhere: The eyes are the window to the soul. Up until that day, I never really thought about that. After this day, I did. I have been surprised by what I see in eyes, time and again. There is this research about 36 questions with a stranger and that you fall in love with them afterwards. What people often don't know, is that part of this experiment you have to look the other in the eyes for 4 minutes at the end. 4 minutes! That is long! The first minute is awkward and you try hard not to laugh. After that, it seems very connecting. Again, I cannot explain it in words, it is special. I highly recommend trying this with a partner.

There are so many more examples, big and small that I cannot fit in this story of 10 minutes. Eyes really are the window to the soul I believe that. If you get the chance to look in somebody's eyes this week, do it. You might be surprised with what you will see.
