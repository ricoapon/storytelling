# My Own Domain

I love programming. I am a real nerd and I love it. Programming has been my hobby for most of my life and it has also become my job. My skills as a programmer have improved significantly since I started working as a Software Engineer. My hobby project became bigger and bigger. It fluctuates a bit how much time outside of work I spend on programming. If it is warm and sunny outside, I am less likely to sit inside. Winter period is definitely my time of the year for hobby projects. I especially love to program when it is raining or storming outside.

When I build things, I like to share that with others. A convenient way to do that is to create a website. I can just send you the link and you can open it. You don't need to install anything. Just open the link on your phone, very convenient. You can hang out with friends anywhere and show it right there and then.

When I publish a website, I do this for free using some large site called GitHub. They have this very cool deal that if you make your code open source, you can publish your website for free. I love free stuff. I am Dutch after all.

There is only one downside: you cannot choose the name of your website. You get some random text and something with github.io. A few years ago I sent a link to my friend, Alain, to test my website. I was making a game and he wanted to try it out. He liked my game and didn't have much feedback on that. However, he didn't like the link. He was like: why don't you buy ricoapon.nl?

I thought that would be a waste of money. I had all these hobby projects, but they were just that: hobby projects. Many were not polished or even finished. Many were just an idea that I spent some time on and then went on to something else when I lost interest. Many were not even worth to be a website in my eyes.

Alain disagreed. He said: "This is one of your biggest hobbies. You spend so much time on all these different websites. It looks a lot more professional if you have your own domain. Besides, you have a job that pays well, so you can surely affort it. How can money ever be an argument?" I had no good answer to that. I thought: "Fuck it, let's just buy it." The same day I bought my own domain. Now I own ricoapon.nl.

Since I made that purchase I made so many websites. I recently counted all the websites that I published and I have a whopping 22 websites! That is a lot! Many are not finished or will ever be finished, but I still think it is cool. I am very happy that Alain convinced me to do this.

And do you know how much it costs to keep all these websites up and running? Only 5 euros a year.
