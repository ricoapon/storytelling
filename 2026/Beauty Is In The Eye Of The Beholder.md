# Beauty Is In The Eye Of The Beholder

I am not attractive. I am not good-looking. I will not get a girlfriend looking like this.

This is what I told myself for a large part of my life. I didn't think I was very ugly, I was just ok. I took care of myself. I always do some sport, so I am physically fit and slim. I shower every day, I wear a clean shirt, I shave or trim my beard, I wear perfume. These are basics that everybody can do (and I think should do, since it's not that hard). But I didn't really care what clothing I wore. I didn't match my shirt with my shoes or pants or whatever. As long as it fits comfortably, I would wear it. I didn't care so much about my appearance, but that was just my coping mechanism I realized later.

A few years ago I became friends with a couple, Rashiq and Marina. I met Rashiq while playing squash. We have a similar mentality on how we approached squash. We wanted to train hard and become the best. We started playing more together and we also started hanging out outside of squash. I also got to meet his wife, Marina. We also clicked, so I saw them more and more. They are both great people and I love being with them. They are very smart and we have stimulating conversations that makes me think. They are also very generous. I live across the street, and I am invited to their place very frequently.

Marina noticed that I would often joke about my appearance in a negative way, in a way that showed I was not confident about myself. I also think these are weird stuff like "nobody is crazy enough to like me" or "it doesn't matter", "I will be alone anyway." So Marina told me she saw this pattern and asked what the deal was with the negativity. This led to an open and vulnerable conversation. I told her about how I was never really popular with girls. I had a girlfriend when I was 19 to 23, and somewhere in the relationship she mentioned that she didn't find me attractive. There was no malice in that, we still loved each other, it was just something we discussed.

After that relationship I was not successful with women at all. I can remember the one time I went speed dating. I saw like 25 women in one evening and almost half of them I liked. Really beautiful women with whom I had a nice conversation. The day after you get an email and I had 0 matches. That shattered any confidence I still had and I didn't date for a longer period afterwards.

Marina listened to my whole story. At the end she responded with only one sentence: "I find you attractive." I didn't know how to respond to that. I don't even remember how I responded... I was shocked... And then... amazed! Somebody found me attractive. Me! I was so vulnerable in that moment. She chose to be so kind. And it really touched me. I felt deep within that it changed me. When I came home from the conversation with Marina I looked in the mirror, and instead of thinking "I am not attractive," I thought "I am attractive."

That did wonders for my confidence. I felt better about myself. I cared more about my appearance and started experimenting more. I tried to find my style in clothing, tried wearing jewelry, I really started to like the way I looked.

Then, I got a girlfriend. And a gorgeous one at that. Best of all, she found me hot. She would just pull me in by my shirt and kiss me. I could just feel that she wanted me. I know confidence comes from within, but dayum does that feel nice. A gorgeous woman wanting you is one of the best feelings in the world.
